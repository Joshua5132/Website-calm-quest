Anxiety:

Managing anxiety is all about building healthy habits, staying active, and practicing relaxation exercises. It is really important for teens to talk about their feelings openly and to get professional help when they need it. There are several ways to help teens manage anxiety, including:

Talking therapies: These include cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) and family therapy.

Lifestyle changes: Getting regular exercise, eating healthy, and cutting back on caffeine.

Relaxation techniques: Mindfulness, meditation, and breathing exercises can help calm the mind.

Medications: In some cases, a doctor may suggest medicine to help, but it will be closely monitored.

Depression:

The WHO suggests that helping teens overcome depression starts with spotting the signs early and getting treatment. This can include support from trained professionals, building strong relationships at home and school, and encouraging healthy habits like regular exercise and good sleep. It is also important to tackle any issues like bullying or school stress and to make sure mental health services are easy to access to help prevent and manage depression.

Culturally appropriate interventions: Utilizing therapy that aligns with cultural values, such as family-centred approaches.

Community support: Engaging community resources to provide a network of assistance.

Addressing social determinants: Tackling underlying issues like poverty that contribute to mental health challenges.

Promoting open conversations: Encouraging discussions about mental health within families and communities.

Improving healthcare access: Ensuring that mental health services are readily available and accessible.

Eating Disorders:

Therapy Types

Treatment often involves evidence-based psychological therapies, including individual, family, and group therapy, as well as nutritional advice and meal planning.

Treatment Settings

Depending on how serious the disorder is, treatment can happen in clinics (either residential or day programs) or hospitals to stabilize health.

Medication

There is not a specific medication for eating disorders, but some can help with symptoms of anxiety or depression.

Specialized Therapies

Family Based Treatment (FBT): Involves parents in the recovery process, effective for teens.

Adolescent Focused Therapy (AFT): Focuses on personal growth in young people.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT-E): Works on changing thoughts and behaviours related to eating disorders.

Integrative Cognitive Affective Therapy (ICAT): Short-term approach to increase emotional awareness before binge episodes.

Maudsley Anorexia Treatment for Adults (MANTRA): Builds emotional skills and challenges unhealthy eating behaviours.

Specialist Supportive Clinical Management (SSCM): Combines therapy and practical support.

Complementary Therapies

Methods like mindfulness, hypnotherapy, and relaxation techniques can also be helpful.

Family Support

Family involvement is super important for recovery. Learning about eating disorders can help family members provide better support.

Suicidal Tendencies:

Promote mental health awareness and education in schools.

Provide access to mental health services, including counselling and support groups.

Encourage open communication about mental health among peers and families.

Implement anti-bullying programs to foster supportive school environments.

Train educators and healthcare providers to recognize and respond to warning signs.
